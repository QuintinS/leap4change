Volunteer Opportunities

Casual Volunteers

You do not have to have a qualification to make a real difference in the life and education of our children. Only an interest in children's education, a willingness to learn and drive to make a difference. As a casual volunteer you are a valuable member of the team. We will train you to teach the children the foundational concepts they need to succeed. You will be hands on providing the support, the care, the attention and the education that some children would otherwise not receive.

Does this sound like you? Volunteer Today!

Technical Volunteers

The organisations Leap4Change work with often need volunteers with special skills. Due to a lack of resources having a volunteer with a technical / academic skill is invaluable. If you have experience or a qualification in a specific area like Monitoring and Evaluation, Graphic design, Occupational therapy, Arts, Sport or Teaching we would love to hear from you. Your mornings will be spent working with the children in the classrooms and afternoons assisting the organisation in your area of expertise. An excellent way to gain experience while benefiting others.

ECD Programme

Brainboosters Programme

The BRAINBOOSTERS programmes focus on early childhood development. They are designed for children aged 1 to 7 years and involve teaching learning through the use of play.

The Brainboosters curriculum is an engaging and interactive method of teaching. The programme focuses on critical foundation concepts in a way that is fun so the children learn through play.

Each programme is intended to help children learn specific concepts or reach certain goals and climb the ladder of learning step=by-step so that they can build a solid cognitive foundation. The modules include colour, shapes, counting and catch-up maths.

Through the continued evaluation of the BrainBoosters programme significant impact has been measured. The improvement in the children's understanding and retention of knowledge is phenomenal. It has been found, for example, that Grade R children can learn 21 letters out of 26 in just 12 weeks with this programme, as opposed to learning 26 letters over a whole year with the current curriculum.

There are programmes to show carers and volunteers how to help children master important building blocks learn at home, in daycare, after school and even in baby clinics. Surrounding children with as much support as possible makes it more likely to succeed. This approach is a new way of thinking about 'problems in South African education', and it works!
