LEAP4Change believes success is easier when children can make the most of their education, and making the most of their education is easier when they've developed basic skills and knowledge.

Most people take these basic tools for granted, but when there are very little resources this solid foundation is hard to acquire. We want you to help us make this privilege the right that it should be. Our vision is to ensure every child is equipped with the building blocks to help them succeed in education, sport and life.

Our team and partners have more than 40 years of combined experience, and we can equip you to be able to make a real difference and contribution to the future and to those around you.

If we all work together, we can really LEAP4Change.

What We Do

Volunteer Placement and Training

Our projects are waiting for volunteers to assist them, and we will give you the appropriate training for what you'll be doing in the time that you have available. Your contribution could range from developing fine motor skills of toddlers through play, or teaching children to count and read, to encouraging teamwork through sport. Are you are a graduate? School leaver? Do you love children? Believe in education? We will match your skills and interests to a project that is a perfect match for you.

Specialist Volunteer and Intern Recruitment

We are always in need of volunteers with special skills to assist our projects. If you have training in education, finance, graphic design, M&E, yoga, music, sport (the list is endless) - we need your skills. You will be matched with a project that really needs your skills, an excellent way to put your education to use for the benefit of others.

Training for Edu-carers, Primary Caregivers and Parents

We offer training and support to Edu-carers, parents, aunties, uncles, grannies and grandads using the booster programme. Anyone who wants to see the children in their care succeed at school can help, and it's never too early to start the learning process. We can provide you with the basic training, materials and ongoing support to give the children in your care a head start in their education.

If you would like to learn more about the Booster programme and how we can help you, contact us or learn more about the programme here.

Who do we work with?

LEAP4Change works with exceptional organisations, which are addressing real needs and making positive contributions. These range from registered NPOs to neighbourhood-run daycare facilities that keep a community's children safe and fed.

Our partners are all focused on developing children in a holistic way, as well as positively impacting the communities they live in.

Learn more about our Partners

The Anna Foundation

The Anna Foundation works with hundreds of children in rural farming communities. Their programmes aim to build each child's sense of self-worth and improve self-esteem. They are seeing gradual, positive changes in the behaviour of the children which is extremely encouraging.

With the Anna Foundation volunteers will receive additional training in the 3R's programme.

3 R'S PROGRAMME: READING, RUNNING, RIGHT-ING. The "3 R's: Reading, Running and Right-ing” programme is based on a philosophy of holistic development and a belief in ‘Healthy Body, Healthy Mind'. This is a professionally designed programme aimed at learners from Grade R to 12 and involves education, sports and life skills programmes. Children attending the after-school do so voluntarily and participate in all aspects of the 3 R's Programme.

Masikhule

Through Masikhule’s will to empower through knowledge, they strive to develop women and children within impoverished communities towards self-reliance, independence and achieving their full potential and thus also the potential of the community as a whole.

Masikhule reaches hundreds of children in ECD centres in the underprivileged communities surrounding Cape Town. Volunteers will be assisting teachers with the day to day classes and together you will teach the children using the booster programme.

Visit Website

Vision Afrika

Vision Afrika works with Children from the ages 2 - 18 years old from the townships of Kayamandi and Vlottenberg in the Stellenbosch wine region. Their aim is to ensure that children not only have access to a great education but also equip them with life skills, personal growth skills, academic and employability skills.

At Vision Afrika volunteers will assist teachers using the booster programme with the younger children in the mornings and will assist with aftercare and homework assistance in the afternoons.

Visit Website

Leap4Change Centres

LEAP4Change works in a number of home run Daycare Centres and Creches. These centres receive educational support only from us. Here we focus on our ‘Play to Learn’ programme for children age 0-4. Volunteers use the interactive games to teach the basic concept the children need in order to ensure that when the time comes to move to formal schools they have a solid foundation to build upon.

Meet the Team

Dominique Maclean - Project Coordinator

Dom started her career in finance and in 2011 made the bold choice to follow her heart and pursue her degree in Social Welfare. In 2013 Dom relocated to Mpumalanga to take on the challenge of Community Development Officer working with rural communities bordering the Kruger National Park. Dominique's work with these communities was largely focused on education, skills development and job creation. Now back in Cape Town, Dom works on innovative projects that will make a REAL difference in the lives of young children.

Kim Cottrell - Advisory Board Member

After completing her Bachelor's degree with majors in English and Psychology, Kim went on to establish two pre-schools in rural Swaziland. Kim's enthusiasm for education in South Africa is highly contagious. In addition to lending her wealth of knowledge and experience to LEAP4Change, Kim is the Head Of Department at Curro Private School as well as a APE facilitator and IEB Moderator.

Bruce Cook - Advisory Board Member

Bruce Cook studied at Pretoria university and progressed to managing large agricultural concerns. He helped the South African citrus industry to gain access to International markets before managing the logistics of moving 1 million tons of fruit from South Africa to the markets of the world. His most recent venture is in green energy where he is involved in Biogas, Solar and wind energy as part of the solutions to the world's future power needs.

Bruce is passionate about innovation and education has always been an important part of his management philosophy.

When looking at South Africa he believes that education is the real solution to our country's future.

Nadia Cooper - General Manager

After many years in corporate Sales and Marketing. Nadia decided to trade in her business suit for a wetsuit and become a diving instructor. This led her to many rural areas in India, South Africa and Mozambique where she saw the need for community upliftment. Nadia's experiences led her to giving water survival lessons to children from local farming communities, basic health and hygiene classes, coordinating Food security and Nutrition clinics, developing environmental projects and supportive early Childhood development programmes. Nadia believes that it is the everyone's responsibility to ensure that our children receive the education they need to become the great leaders of tomorrow.
