Who do we work

LEAP4Change works with exceptional organisations, which are addressing real needs and making positive contributions. These range from registered NPOs to neighbourhood-run daycare facilities that keep a community's children safe and fed.

Our partners are all focused on developing children in a holistic way, as well as positively impacting the communities they live in.

The Anna Foundation

The Anna Foundation works with hundreds of children in rural farming communities. Their programmes aim to build each child's sense of self-worth and improve self-esteem. They are seeing gradual, positive changes in the behaviour of the children which is extremely encouraging.

With the Anna Foundation volunteers will receive additional training in the 3R's programme.

3 R'S PROGRAMME: READING, RUNNING, RIGHT-ING. The "3 R's: Reading, Running and Right-ing” programme is based on a philosophy of holistic development and a belief in ‘Healthy Body, Healthy Mind'. This is a professionally designed programme aimed at learners from Grade R to 12 and involves education, sports and life skills programmes. Children attending the after-school do so voluntarily and participate in all aspects of the 3 R's Programme.

Visit The Anna Foundation's Website

Masikhule

Through Masikhule’s will to empower through knowledge, they strive to develop women and children within impoverished communities towards self-reliance, independence and achieving their full potential and thus also the potential of the community as a whole.

Masikhule reaches hundreds of children in ECD centres in the underprivileged communities surrounding Cape Town. Volunteers will be assisting teachers with the day to day classes and together you will teach the children using the booster programme.

Visit Masikhule's Website

Vision Afrika

Vision Afrika works with Children from the ages 2 - 18 years old from the townships of Kayamandi and Vlottenberg in the Stellenbosch wine region. Their aim is to ensure that children not only have access to a great education but also equip them with life skills, personal growth skills, academic and employability skills.

At Vision Afrika volunteers will assist teachers using the booster programme with the younger children in the mornings and will assist with aftercare and homework assistance in the afternoons.

Visit Vision Afrika's Website

Leap4Change Centres

LEAP4Change works in a number of home run Daycare Centres and Creches. These centres receive educational support only from us. Here we focus on our ‘Play to Learn’ programme for children age 0-4. Volunteers use the interactive games to teach the basic concept the children need in order to ensure that when the time comes to move to formal schools they have a solid foundation to build upon.
